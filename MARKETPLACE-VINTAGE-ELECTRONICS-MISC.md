## Category Strategy

### Vintage electronics buyers

Vintage electronics buyers fall into three groups:

1. **Nostalgia buyers** — want a CD player, Walkman-style item, old camera, etc. because it reminds them of a previous era.
2. **Practical users** — want a simple device for CDs, language learning, audiobooks, old media, or a child/parent.
3. **Collectors / prop buyers** — want sealed/new-old-stock packaging, Y2K display pieces, or period-correct items.

Best copy angle:

```text
new old stock / complete in package / vintage sealed display / untested due to packaging
```

### Accessories buyers

Accessory buyers care about:

- style
- brand/franchise
- function
- giftability
- condition
- whether the item is clean and complete

Best copy angle:

```text
cute, functional, giftable, local pickup, condition shown clearly
```

### TCG buyers

TCG buyers care about:

- authenticity
- condition
- language
- set/card number
- market value
- front/back photos
- whether cards are singles, lots, sealed, graded, or bulk

Best copy angle:

```text
clear photos, accurate condition, no mystery, no fake hype
```

### Motorcycle accessory buyers

Motorcycle buyers care about:

- fitment
- condition
- mounting hardware
- dimensions
- waterproofing/weather wear
- whether all straps/harnesses are included
- local inspection before buying

Best copy angle:

```text
fitment disclosed, hardware included/missing, inspect before buying, public local meetup
```

### eBay condition recommendation

This is tricky.

Because it is unopened but untested, you have two options:

1. **New other / Open box**
   - Better buyer appeal.
   - More risk if buyer claims it does not work.
   - Must clearly say untested due to sealed packaging.

2. **For parts or not working**
   - Safest for seller protection if truly untested.
   - Lower conversion and lower price.

Recommendation:

If you do not want risk, list as:

```text
For parts or not working - New old stock, unopened/untested
```

If you are willing to accept a return/refund if it does not work, list as:

```text
New other / new old stock - untested
```

For local pickup, the buyer can inspect packaging and understand it is untested.

# Future Category: TCG / Pokémon Cards

You mentioned Pokémon cards as a key future category. TCG should be handled differently from general collectibles because buyers are extremely condition-sensitive.

## Best platforms

1. TCGplayer — best for singles once inventory grows
2. eBay — best for graded cards, lots, sealed, higher-end singles
3. Facebook Marketplace — best for local binders/lots
4. OfferUp — local lots, lower-end cards
5. Whatnot — live selling lots and singles once you have volume
6. Mercari — casual card lots, sealed products, Pokémon gift bundles

## TCGplayer photo best practices

TCGplayer’s own guidance recommends clear photos showing the product and condition, including front, back, edges, defects, and unique markings when using photo listings. Photos should not be misleading and should only include items being sold.

Reference:
- https://help.tcgplayer.com/hc/en-us/articles/360000111227-Tips-for-Listings-with-Photos
- https://help.tcgplayer.com/hc/en-us/articles/360000111167-Listings-with-Photos-Policies

## TCG condition checklist

For singles:

- Front photo
- Back photo
- Corners
- Edges
- Surface scratches
- Whitening
- Centering
- Holo scratches
- Dents/creases
- Language
- Set number
- Rarity
- Reverse holo / holo / non-holo

# Final Note

For this category, trust is everything. Vintage electronics and motorcycle accessories can create buyer frustration if something is missing or untested. Your advantage is transparency: disclose what is included, what is missing, what was tested, and what is unknown.
